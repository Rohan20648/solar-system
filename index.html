Welcome to the Solar System

The Planets

Our Solar System is made up of eight planets, each unique in size, atmosphere, and composition.

Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are rocky planets with solid surfaces.

Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn are massive planets mostly made of hydrogen and helium.

Ice Giants

Uranus and Neptune are made of heavier elements like water, methane, and ammonia.

The Moons

Many planets have natural satellites, or moons, orbiting them.

Earth’s Moon

The Moon stabilizes Earth’s tilt and causes tides.

Galilean Moons

Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto orbit Jupiter.

Titan

Saturn’s largest moon has lakes of liquid methane and a thick atmosphere.

About the Solar System

The Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a cloud of gas and dust.
